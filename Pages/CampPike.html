Camp Pike Era

The Camp Pike Era exhibit focuses on the World War I training camp that served as a predecessor to Camp Robinson. Established during World War I, Camp Pike played a crucial role in training Arkansas soldiers for overseas service.

Historical Significance

  • Establishment: Camp Pike was established in 1917 as a major training facility for World War I.
  • Training Operations: Thousands of Arkansas soldiers trained here before deployment to Europe.
  • Post-War Use: The camp continued to serve as a training ground for the National Guard in the interwar period.
  • Artifacts: Uniforms, equipment, and personal stories from Camp Pike's era.

Camp Pike represents Arkansas's significant contribution to the World War I effort.
